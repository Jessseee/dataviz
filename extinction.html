Animals in captivity: Extinct in the Wild

This overview shows which animals are extinct in the wild, only living on in captivity. They are categorized per animal class and continent. Europe is excluded as there are no animals originally from Europe that are extinct in the wild. Data was retrieved from Wikipedia, The IUCN Red List of Threatened Species, and The WWF Wildfinder Database.

Animals in captivity: Consumption

This overview shows the total amount (in tons) of animals used for meat consumption in Europe. This amount is based on the weight of approved meat, meaning more animals were slaughtered than used eventually. This data was retrieved from the EU Open Data Portal. This information is indicative though not completely representative as not all European countries were included due to a lack of data for some (due to confidentiality). These countries were left out in all visualizations, meaning the total weight is higher and the proportions may differ slightly.

The countries that were excluded are: the Netherlands, Luxembourg, Kosovo, North Macedonia, Norway, and Liechtenstein.
